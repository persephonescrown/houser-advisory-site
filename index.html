The Problem

AI pilots don't fail. Launches do.

The tools work and the demos are impressive. Pilots stall because nobody redesigned the workflow, brought the team along, or defined what "done" looks like. That's not a technology problem. It's a change management problem, and it's why AI initiatives stay stuck in pilot mode indefinitely.

The Standing Rule

Autonomy where it is safe. Approval where it matters.

No system Houser Intelligence Advisory ships can send, spend, share, delete, or publish on its own. Every consequential action passes through a named human gate, by design. Clients keep the keys. The systems keep the pace. A gate is a one-tap approval, not a second job. Most take seconds, and they loosen as the system earns trust.

The Method

Common Questions

Frequently Asked

How is this different from just buying AI software?

Software gives a team the tool. Houser Intelligence Advisory does the work most vendors skip: choosing the right workflow, wiring the human gates, and getting the team to use it.

Does this work for a business that isn't a SaaS company?

Yes. The audit runs the same for a two-person shop as it does for a revenue organization. The workflows differ: a shop's are things like listing follow-up, scheduling, and pricing; a revenue team's are lead routing, CRM hygiene, and forecasting. The method does not change.

What if the team doesn't trust AI yet?

That's expected, and the Governed Loop is built around it. Every system ships with a named human gate, so nothing acts on its own. Trust gets built engagement by engagement, not assumed up front.

How long before something is running?

The audit takes days, not months. The first governed system typically ships within a few weeks of the audit, scoped tightly enough to reach production instead of stalling in pilot.

What happens after the engagement ends?

The system keeps running. It's documented and owned by the team, not dependent on Houser Intelligence Advisory to operate. Expansion happens only if the results justify it.
